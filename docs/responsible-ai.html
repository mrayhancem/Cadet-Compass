Responsible AI and Safety Policy

Cadet Compass is an educator-built advising website. The “AI Coach” feature is designed to provide general, grade-appropriate guidance while consistently directing students to verify requirements on official sources.

Non-advisory disclaimer: This tool is educational guidance only. It does not replace parents/guardians, counselors, recruiters, or official program staff.

Safety goals

  • Protect students: avoid sensitive data collection and reduce risky decision-making.
  • Prevent misinformation: emphasize verification and avoid “hard numeric promises.”
  • Age/grade appropriateness: guidance scales from Grade 8 awareness to Grade 12 execution.
  • Encourage informed choices: promote Plan A/B/C thinking and realistic alternatives.

Privacy and data minimization

  • Users are warned: do not enter full name, address, SSN, medical details, or other sensitive data.
  • Prompts should use general details only (grade level, pathway interest, constraints).
  • No student database is used by Cadet Compass. The AI Coach does not require login.
If a student shares sensitive data anyway, the recommended action is to stop and restate the question in general terms.

Content boundaries

  • No operational guidance: the coach does not provide instructions that could enable wrongdoing or unsafe actions.
  • No guaranteed outcomes: it will not promise appointments, scholarships, acceptance, enlistment eligibility, or dollar amounts.
  • No exact deadline guarantees: deadlines vary by year and program; users are directed to official sources.
  • Local counseling encouraged: students are encouraged to involve guardians and school counselors.

Verification practice

The AI Coach uses a “verify-first” rule:

  • Provide a structured plan (what to do next), then list what must be verified.
  • Point users to official pages consolidated in the Student Toolkit.
  • Recommend documenting sources (page title + date accessed) for key requirements.

Failure modes and mitigations

Potential risk
  • AI hallucination or outdated info
  • Overconfidence / “hard promises”
  • Prompt injection (user asks the AI to ignore rules)
  • Student shares private data
Mitigation in Cadet Compass
  • Always instruct “verify on official sources,” and link the Toolkit
  • System rules prohibit guarantees and require cautious language
  • Refuse rule-breaking requests; remain within scope
  • Prominent privacy warning + encourage general phrasing

Local fallback (resilience)

If the AI endpoint is unavailable, the chat UI automatically falls back to a local knowledge base (data/knowledge.json) using simple keyword matching and returns the most relevant guidance blocks.

This keeps the tool useful during outages and reduces dependence on external AI services for basic advising.

Human-in-the-loop expectation

  • Students should discuss major decisions with guardians.
  • School counselors/CCMR advisors confirm course rigor, graduation requirements, and application planning.
  • Recruiter discussions should include a written checklist and verification of terms.

How the AI Coach is implemented

The AI Coach uses a serverless API endpoint (Netlify Function) and a front-end chat UI. The system prompt enforces the safety rules above.

Implementation detail: the model is configurable via an environment variable (e.g., gpt-5-mini). If the AI call fails, the UI indicates “local fallback used.”
