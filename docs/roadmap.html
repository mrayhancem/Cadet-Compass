Grades 8–12

Military Pathway Roadmap

A grade-by-grade checklist for Service Academies, ROTC scholarships, National Guard/Reserve options, and enlistment readiness.

Principle

Start early

For officer pathways, leadership, academics, and fitness compound over time. This roadmap is designed to prevent “late discovery” in Grade 11.

Principle

Always have a Plan B

Cadet Compass teaches students to build a primary plan (e.g., Academy) and a backup plan (e.g., ROTC) and a safety plan (e.g., enlist + later commissioning).

Roadmap at a glance

GradeFocusKey actions
8Explore & planLearn officer vs enlisted; identify interests; start fitness baseline; track activities.
9Build foundationsStrong attendance & grades; leadership roles; join teams/clubs; service hours; meet counselor.
10Position for optionsHigher rigor courses; leadership growth; SAT/ACT planning; academy summer program research.
11Execute applicationsAcademy packets + nominations; ROTC scholarships; medical readiness planning; interview practice.
12Finalize pathwayComplete appointments/scholarships; enlistment contracts if chosen; transition planning; family financial planning.

Important: Requirements and timelines change. This site links to official sources for final verification.

What students miss most often

  • Waiting until Grade 11 to start leadership and fitness.
  • Assuming “good grades” alone are enough for competitive officer programs.
  • Not building multiple pathways (Academy + ROTC + enlistment fallback).
  • Not using official sources to verify current requirements.
