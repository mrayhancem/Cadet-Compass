Enlistment — Serving First, College Later (or Concurrently)

Enlistment is a viable military pathway that allows students to gain job skills, income, and education benefits. College can begin during service or after separation, depending on branch, job, and contract.

Immediate service Paid training Education benefits Officer later possible

What enlistment looks like

Core idea

  • Student enlists directly after high school (or later)
  • Completes Basic Training + job-specific training
  • Serves on Active Duty or in Guard/Reserve
  • May pursue college during or after service

Common reasons students choose this

  • Financial independence
  • Hands-on technical training
  • Clear structure and benefits
  • Later transition to ROTC or officer programs

ASVAB basics (eligibility overview)

The ASVAB determines enlistment eligibility and job qualification. Job scores vary widely; numbers below reflect typical minimum eligibility thresholds, not job guarantees.

Branch Typical Minimum AFQT* Notes
Army 31 Higher scores unlock more technical jobs
Marine Corps 31 Physical readiness emphasized
Navy 35 Many jobs require higher line scores
Air Force 36 Highly competitive; strong academics recommended
Coast Guard 40+ Small service; very competitive
Space Force High / selective Limited access; typically through Air Force processes

*AFQT = Armed Forces Qualification Test score (derived from ASVAB). Always verify current standards with official recruiters.

College and education pathways after enlistment

During service

  • Tuition Assistance (TA)
  • Online or night classes
  • Community college partnerships
  • Service-specific degree programs

After service

  • Post-9/11 GI Bill
  • Housing allowance (BAH)
  • Full-time college attendance
  • Transfer to ROTC or officer routes

Important reality

  • Education timing depends on job and unit
  • Not all jobs allow early college
  • Planning ahead matters

Advising notes for students and families

  • Enlistment contracts are legally binding — read carefully
  • Job selection matters more than branch prestige
  • Keep documentation of training and service
  • Confirm education benefits before signing
